Created in 2011, Dark Souls was the first game in its trilogy and as such intodruced a number of standards for the rest of the series. Dark Souls introduced the setting of Lordran; a dark, gloomy, medieval world full of danger and adventure. The following starting classes you are able to choose from in Dark Souls were carried through into Dark Souls 2 and Dark Souls 3:
  • Warrior
  • Knight
  • Wanderer
  • Thief
  • Bandit
  • Hunter
  • Sorcerer
  • Pyromancer
  • Cleric
  • Deprived
These starting classes encouraged different gameplay styles through differences in starting equipment and stats.
Stats are the core abilities in Soulsborne games that dictate your playstyle. There are 8 core stats in Dark Souls:
  1. Vitality

    2. The stat governing health. Raising your Vitality raises your max health.

  2. Attunement

    3. The stat governing spell usage. Raising your Attunement allows a player to equip more spells

  3. Endurance

    4. The stat governing stamina. Raising your Endurance will allow a player to run and fight longer, as well as equip heavier armour

  4. Strength

    5. The stat governing heavy weapons. Raising your Strength allows a player to equip better heavy weapons and deal more damage with them.

  5. Dexterity

    6. The stat governing finesse weapons. Raising your Dexterity allows a player to equip better finesse weapons and deal more damage with them.

  6. Resistance

    7. The stat governing defense. Raising your Resistance causes a player to take less damage from most sources.

  7. Intelligence

    8. The stat governing sorcery. Raising your Intelligence allows a player to equip better sorceries and deal more damage with them.

  8. Faith

    9. The stat governing miracles. Raising your Faith allows a player to equip better miracles and be more effective with them.
